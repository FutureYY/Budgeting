Budgeting

Budgeting is one of the most important part in financial management.

Video on how to budget

So, what are the main points of budgeting?

The "50 30 20" rule

Splitting your allowance into three portions: 50% for needs, 30% for wants and 20% for savings.
Ideally, this is the perfect ratio for budgeting your allowance as it put one's savings apart before spending on anything else.
However, it is ultimately up to you to change the ratio depending on your requirements.
The most important part of this method is to put one's savings apart before spending on anything else.

Prioritizing your needs and wants

Identify all the "needs" first and ensure that your allowance/income can cover that and the remaining money can be used for some "wants".
Before you spend on any "wants", just ensure that you've covered your priorities first.
A "need" is something that is needed to survive while a "want" is something that an individual desires, but would be able to live without.
For example:
Say you already have a laptop, but you convince yourself that a tablet is a "need" because it can make studying easier. This is another example of transforming a "want" into a "need"
This method enables you to prioritize and figure out what expenses are necessary and which ones can be deferred.
